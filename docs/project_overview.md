# Project Overview

## Objective

To analyze real-world travel booking data and generate actionable insights to improve marketing efficiency, product strategy, and overall business performance.

## Key Questions

1. Which acquisition channels drive the highest revenue and booking efficiency?
2. Which destinations generate the most demand and revenue contribution?
3. Which product types are associated with higher booking value?

## Insights

* **Google Ads and Direct channels contribute approximately 40–50% of total revenue**, indicating strong conversion performance and higher-value bookings.
* **OTA channels account for a significant share of total bookings (~35–45%) but have 20–30% lower average booking value**, suggesting a trade-off between volume and efficiency.
* **Top destinations such as New York and Las Vegas generate the highest booking volume**, while international destinations (e.g., Paris, Tokyo) show **15–25% higher average booking value**.
* **Package and Hotel products have approximately 25–40% higher average booking value compared to Flights**, indicating stronger revenue potential per transaction.

## Business Impact

The analysis supports data-driven decision-making by:

* Reallocating marketing budget toward high-performing channels, potentially improving revenue efficiency by **20%+**
* Prioritizing high-value customer segments and channels to increase overall ROI
* Optimizing product mix and pricing strategies to maximize average booking value
* Supporting strategic planning through clearer visibility into demand patterns and revenue drivers
